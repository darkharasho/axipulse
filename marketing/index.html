// KEY FEATURES

Per-fight Pulse

Damage, DPS, downs, strips, cleanses, healing — ranked against your squad. AxiPulse detects your role and surfaces the stats that matter for it.

Second-by-second timeline

Scrub through a fight. Damage dealt, damage taken, distance to tag, healing, boons, CC, down events — all on synchronized lanes.

Top-down map replay

Watch the squad move on the actual WvW map. Position, downs, and engagement flow plotted with profession color-coding and live boon state.

Searchable history

Every parsed fight is saved. Scan a whole night at a glance, click any fight, the full Pulse / Timeline / Map loads instantly.

Local-only parsing

Logs never leave your machine. Elite Insights runs as a local subprocess — no uploads, no accounts, no telemetry.

Automatic Elite Insights management

AxiPulse downloads, installs, and updates Elite Insights for you. On Linux it handles the .NET runtime too. You never touch a CLI or a config file — the parser just works.

// FAQ
Does AxiPulse upload my logs anywhere?

No. Everything is parsed locally with Elite Insights running as a subprocess on your machine. Nothing is uploaded, no account is required, no telemetry is collected.

Does it touch the game?

No. AxiPulse only reads .evtc files arcdps writes to disk after a fight ends. Nothing is injected into Guild Wars 2.

What is Elite Insights, and do I need to install it?

Elite Insights is the open-source parser that turns .evtc files into structured fight data. AxiPulse downloads, installs, and keeps it up-to-date for you — and handles the .NET runtime on Linux too.

Will it slow my game down?

Parsing happens on a background process after the fight ends. Drop AxiPulse on a second monitor and it stays out of your way during combat.

Can I use AxiPulse on Linux?

Yes. Linux builds ship as an AppImage. AxiPulse installs and manages the .NET runtime Elite Insights needs — you don't have to touch any of it.

Is it free?

Yes. Free forever, no paid tier.
